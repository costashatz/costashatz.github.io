Profile

I am currently a PhD candidate in Robotics and Machine Learning at Inria Nancy, Larsen Team, under the supervision of Dr. Jean-Baptiste Mouret. My work focuses on studying and providing abilities to autonomous robots to be able to compensate for unknown damages using machine learning techniques and evolutionary computation. This work is part of the ERC "ResiBots" project.

        

My first contact with a computer was at the age of 7-8. At first, I was only playing games, but I was rather quickly drawn to programming and problem solving. I started programming at 12 and have never stopped since. My skills in mathematics and my quick adaptation helped me grasp the principles of programming and problem solving at a young age. Consequently, I pursued studies in Computer Engineering and I chose Computer Engineering and Informatics Department (CEID) of University of Patras, the most complete computer engineering department in Greece. I graduated from the CEID on 20th November 2015, with 8.25/10 GPA (5% of my class). In the first years in CEID, I acquired strong mathematical and computer engineering foundations. In the next couple of years, I was attracted to Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning and Computer Graphics. In the 3rd year, I came across Lego's Mindstorms robotics kit in AI group in CEID and I experimented a little building small wheeled robots. My love for Robotics had just started. In the 4th year, I came across "A.I. for Robotics" online course offered by Udacity and this field captivated me for good. By the end of the 4th year of my studies, I was certain that the intersection of Robotics and Artificial Intelligence would be my main field of interest/research. In addition, I have attended and completed over 15 online courses covering a very broad range of topics, at Udacity, Coursera and edX. These online courses in addition to 66 in-campus courses have given to me opportunities to develop both my analytical and academic skills, as I had to complete several quizzes and one or more (programming) projects for each course.

            

My academic interests lie in the areas of Robotics, Robot Locomotion, Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, 2D/3D Computer Graphics, Computer Vision, and Software Engineering. I love developing software for robots and studying robotic mechanisms. I am also interested in developing software for "smart" and adaptive behaviors in robotic applications. What really captivates me is the applicability of the above areas; you discover something and you can try it in the real world (almost) immediately.
